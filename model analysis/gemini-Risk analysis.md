# LEGAL RISK ANALYSIS: APEX COMMERCIAL DEVELOPMENT (PHASE 2)
**Prepared for:** SMART Balustrades Limited (Subcontractor)
**Document Reference:** test_contract_jct.pdf Analysis

---

## CONTRACT OVERVIEW
* **Type:** JCT Design and Build Subcontract, 2024 Edition (Amended)
* **Parties:** Summit Build Solutions Limited (Contractor) & SMART Balustrades Limited (Subcontractor)
* **Value:** GBP 287,500
* **Jurisdiction:** England and Wales
* **Summary:** This contract is extremely one-sided and heavily amended in favour of the Contractor. It strips away standard JCT risk protections, introduces unlimited liabilities for the Subcontractor, and severely restricts your statutory rights under UK law. In its current form, it represents a critical commercial risk to your business.

---

## CRITICAL RISKS

### Risk 1: Uncapped Indemnity and Unlimited Liability
* **Severity:** Critical
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 5.1
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** You are required to indemnify the Contractor and Employer against all losses (including indirect, consequential, and loss of profit) without any financial cap. If a major delay or defect occurs, you could face claims that far exceed your total subcontract sum of £287,500, exposing your business to insolvency. It also covers losses "whether or not reasonably foreseeable", which severely extends your liability beyond standard common law.
* **Statutory Conflict:** Potentially conflicts with the **Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 (UCTA)** regarding reasonableness, particularly as the Contractor heavily limits their own liability in Clause 5.2.

### Risk 2: Fitness for Purpose Design Warranty
* **Severity:** Critical
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 2.1
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** You are guaranteeing that your design will be "fit for its intended purpose". Standard Professional Indemnity (PI) insurance policies only cover "reasonable skill and care" and expressly exclude "fitness for purpose" warranties. If a design issue arises, your PI insurance will likely refuse the claim, leaving you to fund 100% of the defense and rectification costs out of pocket.

### Risk 3: Severe Limitation on Contractor's Liability
* **Severity:** High
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 5.2
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** While your liability is completely uncapped under Clause 5.1, the Contractor has capped their entire liability to you at a mere £10,000. If the Contractor causes a massive delay, breaches the contract, or wrongfully terminates, the maximum you can ever recover from them is £10,000, regardless of your actual financial loss.
* **Statutory Conflict:** Strongly conflicts with **UCTA 1977**, as such an asymmetric cap between commercial parties is highly likely to fail the statutory "test of reasonableness".

### Risk 4: Outlawed Set-Off and Deduction Rights
* **Severity:** High
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 3.2 & Clause 4.2
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** Clause 3.2 allows the Contractor to withhold money without giving you any prior notice. Furthermore, Clause 4.2 allows them to deduct Liquidated and Ascertained Damages (LADs) without issuing a Pay Less Notice. This completely destroys your cash flow security.
* **Statutory Conflict:** Directly violates **Section 111 of the Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996**. Under UK law, a contractor *must* issue a valid Pay Less Notice before withholding any sum otherwise due. Clause 4.2 and parts of Clause 3.2 are legally ineffective and void.

### Risk 5: Exclusion of Interest on Late Payments
* **Severity:** Medium
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 3.1
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** The contract states "No interest shall be payable by the Contractor on any late payment". This removes any financial incentive for the Contractor to pay you on time, and forces you to fund cash flow gaps for free.
* **Statutory Conflict:** Directly violates the **Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998**. You have a statutory right to claim interest (currently 8% above the Bank of England base rate) plus compensation on late commercial payments. Any contract clause attempting to exclude this right is void unless a "substantial remedy" is provided instead.

### Risk 6: Uncapped Liquidated Damages (LADs)
* **Severity:** High
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 4.1
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** LADs are set at £2,500 per week or part thereof with "no cap on the aggregate amount". In an 11-week project, a prolonged delay could result in LADs completely wiping out your contract value. Standard subcontracts cap LADs at 5% to 10% of the contract sum.

### Risk 7: No Claim for Concurrently Working Trades
* **Severity:** Medium
* **Clause Reference:** Clause 2.3
* **Impact on Subcontractor:** You waive all rights to claim for delay, disruption, or additional costs caused by other subcontractors or the Contractor's programming. If another trade blocks your access or delays your installation window, you must absorb those acceleration or delay costs completely.

## KEY TERMS SUMMARY
* **Payment terms:** Monthly in arrears. Application on the last working day of the month; Payment Notice within 14 days of receipt; Final payment due within 45 days of application. No late payment interest.
* **Retention:** 5% held until Practical Completion of the Subcontract Works; reduced to 2.5% thereafter until the expiry of the Defects Liability Period. Not held in trust (at risk if Contractor goes bust).
* **Indemnity:** Uncapped, fully loaded indemnity from Subcontractor covering all foreseeable and unforeseeable direct/indirect losses.
* **Insurance:** Public Liability: £5,000,000 per occurrence; Employer's Liability: £10,000,000; Professional Indemnity: £1,000,000 per claim (To be maintained during the works and for 12 years thereafter).
* **Notice requirements:** 28-day strict condition precedent notice required from the Subcontractor for any claims regarding additional payment or Extensions of Time (EoT).
* **Design liability:** Full design responsibility, including structural calculations and fixings. Carries an onerous "fitness for purpose" warranty.
* **Any unusual or onerous terms:** Clause 6.1 allows the Contractor to terminate for convenience (for no reason) on 7 days' notice, while expressly denying you any recovery for loss of profit or overheads on unexecuted works. Clause 5.2 caps Contractor liability at £10,000.

## NEGOTIATION WORDING

### For Clause 2.1 (Design & Fitness for Purpose)
> "Delete Clause 2.1 in its entirety and replace with: 'The Subcontractor shall be responsible for the design of the Subcontract Works as described in Specification C-14. The Subcontractor warrants that in the performance of its design obligations it has exercised, and will continue to exercise, the reasonable skill, care and diligence to be expected of a professionally qualified and competent specialist design subcontractor experienced in carrying out design work for works of a similar size, scope, nature and complexity to the Subcontract Works. Under no circumstances shall the Subcontractor be subject to a fitness for purpose warranty, whether express or implied.'"

### For Clause 3.1 & 3.2 & 4.2 (Payment, Set-Off & Pay Less Notices)
> "Amend Clause 3.1 to insert at the end of the paragraph: 'Interest on late payments shall accrue and be payable in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.' 
> 
> Delete Clause 3.2 and 4.2 and replace with: 'The Contractor may only deduct or set off sums from payments due to the Subcontractor if the Contractor has issued a valid Payment Notice or Pay Less Notice in strict compliance with the Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996 (as amended). Any such notice must specify the sum considered to be due, the basis on which that sum is calculated, and the specific contractual grounds for withholding.'"

### For Clause 4.1 (LADs Cap)
> "Amend Clause 4.1 by adding the following text to the end of the clause: 'Provided always that the aggregate liability of the Subcontractor for liquidated and ascertained damages under this Subcontract shall be strictly capped at a maximum of 10% of the Subcontract Sum (GBP 28,750).'"

### For Clause 5.1 & 5.2 (Indemnity, Mutual Liability Cap, and Consequential Loss)
> "Delete Clause 5.1 and 5.2 in their entirety and replace with: 
> '(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Subcontract, the total aggregate liability of either Party to the other under or in connection with this Subcontract, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty or otherwise, shall be strictly limited to 100% of the Subcontract Sum, namely GBP 287,500.
> (b) Neither Party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, special or consequential loss or damage, including but not limited to loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of business, or loss of production.
> (c) The financial caps and exclusions set out in this Clause 5 shall not apply to liabilities arising from death or personal injury caused by negligence, or fraud.'"

## RECOMMENDATIONS

* **Overall Risk Rating:** Critical

### Top negotiation priorities in order:
1. **Remove the Fitness for Purpose Warranty:** Align design liability to "Reasonable Skill and Care" so your Professional Indemnity Insurance is valid.
2. **Insert a Mutual Liability Cap and Exclude Consequential Losses:** Cap your total exposure at 100% of the contract value (£287,500) and delete the asymmetric £10,000 Contractor cap.
3. **Cap Liquidated Damages (LADs):** Establish a 10% cap (£28,750) on delay damages instead of leaving them completely unlimited.
4. **Fix Statutory Compliance (Payment/Set-Off):** Enforce the mandatory use of statutory Pay Less Notices and reinstate your right to Late Payment Interest.
5. **Amend the Termination for Convenience Clause:** Ensure that if they terminate you for no reason, they must pay for your committed costs, demobilisation, and lost profit on the remaining work.

### Actionable next steps before signing:
1. **Send the Negotiation Wording:** Issue the amended language provided above to the Contractor's commercial team immediately. Do not start works on site until these terms are resolved.
2. **Consult Your Broker:** Forward Clause 5.3 and your drafted amendments to your PI Insurance broker to verify they will provide coverage for the 12-year run-off period based on the final agreed text.
3. **Demand the JCT Comparison Document:** Request the formal comparison document referenced in the contract text to ensure no other hidden amendments have been made to the standard JCT DBSub/A 2024 form.
